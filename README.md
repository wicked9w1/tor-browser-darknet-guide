## What is the Tor Browser?
The Tor Browser is a free, open-source tool designed to enable anonymous browsing on the internet. Launched by the Tor Project in 2002, it routes your traffic through a network of volunteer-operated servers called nodes, encrypting data multiple times to protect your privacy. In 2026, with increasing surveillance concerns, Tor remains essential for journalists, activists, and privacy enthusiasts.

Key features include:
- **Onion Routing**: Hides your IP address by bouncing connections through relays.
- **NoScript Integration**: Blocks scripts that could track you.
- **Portable Version**: Runs from a USB drive without installation.

Downloading Tor from the official site (torproject.org) ensures safety—avoid third-party sources to prevent malware.
